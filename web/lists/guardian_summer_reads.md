#### [Transcription](https://books.rob.me.uk/book/2020)
[![](covers/2020.jpg)](https://books.rob.me.uk/book/2020)
##### cerebral · existential · modern
##### Ben Lerner
##### Fiction, 2024, 256 pages

> The Guardian says: "A middle-aged writer returns to his college town to record the final interview with his 90-year-old intellectual mentor. But he’s broken his phone, and doesn’t seem able to confess that it’s not recording … this anxiety dream of a beginning leads us into a series of sharp insights into family, memory, inheritance and storytelling – all that it means to be human, and how smartphones are changing our sense of the world at every level."

Lerner’s distinctively hyperspecific prose style perfectly captures the dizzying paralysis of contemporary life, appealing directly to fans of deeply introspective auto-fiction. 

A broken screen spirals into a profound crisis of memory and mortality. Dive into the brilliant, neurotic mind of an artist desperately trying to hit record on his own legacy before time runs out.

#### Land
##### evocative · sweeping · roots
##### Maggie O’Farrell
##### Fiction, 2024, 384 pages

> The Guardian says: "Following Hamnet and The Marriage Portrait, O’Farrell’s latest historical novel has a personal connection. Inspired by an Irish ancestor who drew up maps for the English in the aftermath of the great famine, it builds into a multigenerational tale of folklore, migration and the meaning of home."

O'Farrell weaves a rich, lyrical tapestry of historical world-building, cementing her reputation as a master of sensory, atmospheric prose that will captivate lovers of literary genealogy.

Maps don't just divide earth; they sever bloodlines. Follow a brilliant cartographer through the devastating aftermath of the famine in a saga where every drawn line carries the weight of a stolen homeland.

#### [Yesteryear](https://books.rob.me.uk/book/1189)
[![](covers/1189.jpg)](https://books.rob.me.uk/book/1189)
##### satirical · high-concept · gritty
##### Caro Claire Burke
##### Fiction, 2024, 320 pages

> The Guardian says: "A social media influencer pushing a confected olden-days lifestyle of babies, baking and prairie dresses wakes up in the actual olden days – to dirt, poverty and domestic abuse. Critics have argued about a lack of political depth in this buzzy tradwife debut, but there’s no denying the power of the high-concept hook – or the furious energy of the voice."

Burke wields a razor-sharp, furious narrative voice, deploying biting satire to dismantle romanticized aesthetics with visceral, grounding details that will thrill fans of dark, speculative contemporary fiction.

Forget the pastel filters of the modern tradwife. Plunged into the brutal, unwashed reality of the actual past, one influencer is about to discover that surviving history takes more than sourdough starter and a prairie dress.

#### John of John
##### tender · insular · lyrical
##### Douglas Stuart
##### Fiction, 2024, 400 pages

> The Guardian says: "In the much-anticipated new novel from the Booker-winning Shuggie Bain author, young Cal returns from art school to his childhood home on the Hebridean island of Harris, and his fervently religious father, John. Both men are keeping secrets in a heartfelt and gorgeously wrought tapestry of faith, isolation, community and gay love."

Stuart builds an impeccably textured atmosphere of remote Scottish isolation, utilizing empathetic, gorgeously wrought prose to explore the devastating intersections of faith and sexuality.

Two deeply estranged men are thrown into an emotional crucible on the wind-battered Hebridean coast. In this haunting exploration of fatherhood and forbidden love, the loudest secrets are the ones hidden in plain sight.

#### [The Palm House](https://books.rob.me.uk/book/2022)
[![](covers/2022.jpg)](https://books.rob.me.uk/book/2022)
##### wry · domestic · sharp
##### Gwendoline Riley
##### Fiction, 2024, 224 pages

> The Guardian says: "Riley excels at dysfunctional family relationships, but is on gentler form here with the story of a long friendship between two prickly souls: Putnam, ageing out of relevance, and Laura, making her precarious way through midlife. It’s a wry take on the comforts of comradeship and the frustrations of other people, with the prose – as ever – sharp as a knife."

Riley executes a masterclass in understated character study, leveraging painfully sharp dialogue and keen psychological realism to attract readers who savor quiet, devastating literary fiction.

Friendship is rarely simple, and getting older certainly isn't. Watch two spectacularly prickly souls navigate the indignities of midlife in a brilliantly observed comedy of manners that stings as much as it soothes.

#### [The Infamous Gilberts](https://books.rob.me.uk/book/2023)
[![](covers/2023.jpg)](https://books.rob.me.uk/book/2023)
##### eccentric · cozy · nostalgic
##### Angela Tomaski
##### Fiction, 2024, 350 pages

> The Guardian says: "An irresistible old-fashioned comfort read about the slow demise of an eccentric family trapped in a crumbling stately home, as seen through their possessions and mementoes when the house is sold off, ready to be turned into a hotel. Romantic misadventures, dastardly characters, lost boys and lashings of atmosphere: it’s all here."

Tomaski crafts an immersive, antique tone that balances lighthearted mischief with melancholic decay, offering an irresistible retreat for readers seeking character-driven, classic estate dramas.

Every crumbling manor has its ghosts, but this one is selling them to the highest bidder. Unpack the scandalous heirlooms of a destitute dynasty in a charmingly chaotic romp through high-society ruin.

#### Honey
##### provocative · feminist · blood-soaked
##### Imani Thompson
##### Fiction, 2024, 310 pages

> The Guardian says: "Thompson’s buzzy debut follows Yrsa, a Black PhD student who embarks on a series of killings targeting men, each murder reminiscent of the violence more typically inflicted on women. A serial-killer thriller with a side of feminist theory, Thompson’s alchemical fusion of racial politics and “weird girl” fiction feels genuinely fresh."

Thompson engineers a ruthless, pulse-pounding pacing intertwined with fierce ideological critique, forging a wholly original crossover that will grip both thriller junkies and literary feminists.

Vengeance has a brand new syllabus. Follow a brilliant, deadly PhD student as she turns the horrifying statistics of gendered violence back on the perpetrators in a slasher that’s as intellectually rigorous as it is terrifying.

#### The Children
##### slippery · traumatic · dark
##### Melissa Albert
##### Fiction, 2024, 336 pages

> The Guardian says: "A bestselling children’s author writes her own kids into her much-loved fantasy series – then dies in mysterious circumstances. As adults, Guin and Ennis must reconcile to confront their traumatic legacy. Set between an isolated house in the woods in 1990s Vermont and present-day New York, this slippery investigation into the price of creativity is a dark fairytale."

Albert channels a deeply unsettling, gothic tone to interrogate the ethics of art, masterfully blending dual timelines to ensnare readers of sophisticated, psychological dark fantasy.

What happens when your mother’s legendary fairy tales ruin your actual life? Two siblings must venture back into the haunted woods of their childhood to untangle the deadly secrets behind the world's favorite bedtime stories.

#### Villa Coco
##### sun-drenched · joyful · eccentric
##### Andrew Sean Greer
##### Fiction, 2024, 288 pages

> The Guardian says: "A sunny holiday read about a young American falling for the delights of Italy when he takes a job as assistant to a 92-year-old aristocrat at the eponymous mansion in the Tuscan hills. Full of eccentric characters, it’s a love letter to friendship and discovery."

Greer orchestrates a buoyant, effervescent prose style that bathes the reader in sensory delight, crafting the ultimate escapist fiction for fans of warm, character-rich travelogues.

Trade the grind for the Tuscan sun. Step inside a rambling Italian estate where a lost American and a ninety-two-year-old aristocrat discover that the best adventures often begin well past the expiration date.

#### A Bad, Bad Place
##### grim · authentic · slow-burn
##### Frances Crawford
##### Fiction, 2024, 368 pages

> The Guardian says: "This slow-burn crime debut set in 1979 Glasgow begins when 12-year-old Janey, walking her dog Sid Vicious, comes across a body. Told in alternating chapters from Janey and her Nana, it’s a pitch-perfect depiction of time and place."

Crawford meticulously constructs a suffocating, authentic historical atmosphere, using brilliant dual perspectives to anchor a procedural that will resonate powerfully with fans of gritty Scottish noir.

In the grey, hopeless sprawl of 1979 Glasgow, a little girl and her dog stumble upon a corpse. Uncover the terrifying truths of a broken city through the eyes of a child who sees far more than the adults want to admit.

#### Prestige Drama
##### sparkling · tragicomic · ensemble
##### Seamus O’Reilly
##### Fiction, 2024, 304 pages

> The Guardian says: "A major new TV series about the Troubles is to be filmed in Derry – but its Hollywood star has gone missing. The first novel from the author of the tragicomic memoir Did Ye Hear Mammy Died? is a sparkling ensemble portrait of a city still dealing with its difficult past."

O’Reilly delivers a masterfully tuned tragicomic voice, balancing razor-sharp banter with profound historical sensitivity in a way that will enchant lovers of intelligent, community-focused satire.

Hollywood has descended on Derry, and it's an unmitigated disaster. When an A-list actor vanishes into the chaotic streets of a recovering city, the locals must navigate the absurd collision of traumatic history and shallow show business.

#### Nonesuch
##### magical · romantic · thrilling
##### Francis Spufford
##### Fiction, 2024, 416 pages

> The Guardian says: "In a London on the brink of the second world war, an ambitious young woman working in the City stumbles into a magical realm of angels, time travel, possible worlds – and a fascist conspiracy to assassinate Winston Churchill and usher in a Nazi future for Britain. Packed with adventure and romance, this delightful fantasy has teeth – a very grown-up pleasure."

Spufford brilliantly marries historical tension with expansive, sophisticated fantasy mechanics, offering a luscious, high-stakes romp perfectly tailored for readers of erudite, adult speculative fiction.

History hangs by a thread of magic. Join a fearless young woman on a dimension-hopping race against time to thwart a fascist conspiracy and save Winston Churchill in a dizzying blend of espionage and the occult.

#### [Black Bag](https://books.rob.me.uk/book/2024)
[![](covers/2024.jpg)](https://books.rob.me.uk/book/2024)
##### absurd · comical · theatrical
##### Luke Kennard
##### Fiction, 2024, 240 pages

> The Guardian says: "As part of a psychology experiment, a struggling actor agrees to appear in university lectures zipped into a large black leather bag with only his feet sticking out, and see how the students react. From this premise, Kennard spins a charming and comical discourse on the absurdity of modern life, riffing on everything from the perilous state of the arts to masculinity and the disappearing horizon of adulthood."

Kennard deploys a brilliantly deadpan, theatrical prose style to dissect contemporary masculinity, resulting in a surreal, philosophically rich comedy that will charm fans of experimental campus novels.

An actor, a leather bag, and the crushing weight of modern existence. What starts as a bizarre university stunt morphs into a hilarious, poignant interrogation of art, survival, and what it actually means to be seen.

#### The Daffodil Days
##### lyrical · ambitious · luminous
##### Helen Bain
##### Fiction, 2024, 380 pages

> The Guardian says: "A lyrical, impeccably researched reimagining of the final year in the marriage of Sylvia Plath and Ted Hughes, as seen by their friends and neighbours in the small Dartmoor town they escape to from London. With multiple narrators and a reverse chronology, it’s an ambitious and impressively achieved debut, and a luminous portrait of the effect literature’s most famous couple had on the people around them."

Bain executes a complex reverse-chronological structure with stunning poetic grace, constructing a voyeuristic yet deeply empathetic historical landscape for devotees of literary biography and tragic romance.

Look past the myth of literature's most doomed marriage. Witness the final, devastating year of Plath and Hughes through the captivated, horrified eyes of the ordinary neighbors who watched their genius and their destruction unfold.

#### Cast Away
##### gripping · psychological · maritime
##### Francesca de Tores
##### Fiction, 2024, 400 pages

> The Guardian says: "Alexander Selkirk, 18th-century privateer and the real-life inspiration for Robinson Crusoe, is marooned on an island in the South Pacific with only a Bible and a cask of booze for company. What follows is a fantastically fresh, gripping adventure story and psychological journey combined, as over the years he develops his survival skills and faces up to his isolation."

De Tores renders a visceral, hyper-realistic survivalist atmosphere, leveraging sparse, urgent prose to chart the profound mental unravelling and resilience of a man trapped at the edge of the world.

Strip away civilization, and what is left of a man? Experience the brutal, astonishing true story of the privateer who inspired Robinson Crusoe as he wages a decades-long war against madness, starvation, and absolute solitude.

#### I Want You to Be Happy

##### Jem Calder

#### The Things We Never Say
##### wise · tender · observational
##### Elizabeth Strout
##### Fiction, 2024, 288 pages

> The Guardian says: "The author who brought us Lucy Barton and Olive Kitteridge introduces us to Artie Dam, a fifty­something teacher struggling to make sense of his troubled marriage and a changing United States, in her wise and tender 11th novel."

Strout delivers her trademark quiet realism, using sparse, deeply intuitive characterizations to build a profound, melancholy resonance perfect for aficionados of character-driven Americana.

#### Wimmy Road Boyz
##### kinetic · slangy · volatile
##### Sufiyaan Salam
##### Fiction, 2024, 250 pages

> The Guardian says: "Set over one chaotic night on Manchester’s Curry Mile, Salam’s debut is a turbo-charged coming-of-age novel following three young British-Pakistani men hurtling towards trouble in a white BMW. Salam electrifies the page with a raw, propulsive vernacular rhythm, capturing the volatile energy of urban youth culture in a tour-de-force that redefines contemporary British street literature.

One night, one white BMW, and zero brakes. Ride shotgun through Manchester's Curry Mile in a blistering, high-octane collision of Gen-Z bravado, brotherhood, and impending disaster.

#### Glyph
